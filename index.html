Jollof Rice and Dodo

One of the most delicious and well-known foods provided at parties and celebrations all over Nigerial is Jollof rice and dodo.
The popularity of this food cuts across most ethnic groups and tribes all over Nigeria.

For those who are not really used to the Yoruba language, "dodo" simply means fried plantain.
Well, if you don't know what plantain is, you seriously need to do some research.

Okay, lets get down to business! What I am going to do first is to list the ingredients necessary to make the dish.

Ingredients Used

  • Long grain rice
  • Ground fresh pepper
  • Sliced Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Groundnut oil
  • Maggi cubes as required
  • Shrimps
  • Cooking butter
  • Ripe plantain
  • Seasoning (curry powder & thyme) to taste
  • Some salt

These are ingredients that are not too hard to come by, and so, get them ready as we run through the procedure

Cooking Procedure

  1. Put some water on fire according to the quantity of the rice.
  2. Add the fresh pepper, maggi, curry & thyme, butter as well as some groundnut oil.
  3. Put the mixture to the boil and then add the rice.
  4. Let the rice cook for about 10 minutes and then add the onions and tomatoes
  5. Allow the rice to cook for about 20 more minutes and then add salt to taste.
  6. Lower the heat and let the rice simmer for about 5 more minutes.
  7. Peel the plantain and slice it thinly.
  8. Put a frying pan on a low fire. Pour the oil into the frying and heat the oil
  9. Put the pieces of sliced plantain into the hot oil and fry until golden brown

So there, we're done! Food is ready to serve!!
